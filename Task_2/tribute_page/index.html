Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

1931 - 2015

"All of us every single year, we're a different person. I don't think we're the same person all our lives."

Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India," Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's space and missile programs.

Dr. Abdul Kalam earned his doctorate in aerospace engineering and became the principal scientific adviser to the Government of India. His vision and dedication transformed India into a space-faring nation and made it self-reliant in defense technology.

Major Achievements

Space Program Leadership

Led India's space program as Director of ISRO, contributing to the development of Indian satellite launch vehicles and orbital platforms.

Missile Development

Chief architect of India's missile development program. Led the development of Agni and Prithvi missiles, making India a missile power.

Pokhran Nuclear Tests

Principal scientific adviser during India's second nuclear tests at Pokhran in 1998, establishing India as a nuclear power.

Presidency of India

Served as the 11th President of India from 2002-2007, known as the "People's President" for his inspiring leadership.

Integrated Guided Missile Development Program

Conceived and directed the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP) to develop indigenous missiles for India's defense.

Educational Reforms

Championed science and technology education in India and inspired millions of students through his lectures and vision for "India 2020".

Awards & Recognition

  • Padma Bhushan (1981) - Third highest civilian award of India
  • Padma Vibhushan (1997) - Second highest civilian award of India
  • Bharat Ratna (1997) - Highest civilian award of India
  • Honorary Doctorates - From multiple universities worldwide
  • Ramakrishnan Medal - From The Institution of Engineers (India)
  • Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration (1997)
  • Elected Fellow - Indian National Academy of Engineering
